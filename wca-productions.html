What is the World Cube Association?

The World Cube Association (WCA) is an organization dedicated to promoting and regulating the sport of speedcubing, also known as speedsolving. Founded in 2004, it is responsible for setting and maintaining the official world records for solving various types of Rubik's Cubes and other twisty puzzles.

The WCA plays a vital role in the speedcubing community by standardizing rules and regulations, organizing competitions, and providing a platform for competitors to connect and share knowledge. Through its global network of delegates and volunteers, the WCA is committed to fostering the growth and development of speedcubing worldwide.

The association has played a significant role in popularizing speedcubing, making it a recognized competitive sport. It has also contributed to the development of new solving techniques and strategies, pushing the boundaries of human dexterity and problem-solving abilities.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

This section provides answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about the World Cube Association (WCA).

Question 1: What is the WCA?


The World Cube Association (WCA) is the international governing body for the sport of speedcubing. Founded in 2004, the WCA is responsible for setting and maintaining the official world records for solving various types of Rubik's Cubes and other twisty puzzles.

Question 2: What are the goals of the WCA?


The WCA's primary goals are to promote and regulate the sport of speedcubing, standardize rules and regulations, organize competitions, and maintain world records.

Question 3: Who can join the WCA?


Anyone can join the WCA by becoming a member. Membership is open to individuals of all ages and backgrounds who share a passion for speedcubing.

Question 4: How can I participate in WCA competitions?


To participate in WCA competitions, you must first become a WCA member. Once you are a member, you can register for competitions through the WCA website.

Question 5: What are the different types of WCA competitions?


The WCA organizes various types of competitions, including single-solve competitions, average-of-five competitions, and blindfolded competitions.

Question 6: How are WCA world records set and verified?


WCA world records are set at official WCA competitions. To set a world record, a competitor must solve a puzzle in a time that is faster than the current world record. The record is then verified by a WCA delegate.
