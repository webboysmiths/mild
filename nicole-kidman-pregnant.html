Fertility and Age

The potential pregnancy of Nicole Kidman at 55 years old highlights the challenges and complexities surrounding fertility and advanced maternal age. As women age, their fertility naturally declines, and the risk of pregnancy complications increases.

Advanced maternal age is defined as pregnancy after the age of 35, and it carries with it an increased risk of:

  • Miscarriage
  • Preeclampsia
  • Gestational diabetes
  • Cesarean delivery
  • Preterm birth
  • Low birth weight
  • Birth defects

Despite these risks, many women over the age of 35 are choosing to have children. This trend is due in part to factors such as:

  • Increased life expectancy
  • Delayed childbearing due to career or educational pursuits
  • Advances in fertility treatments
